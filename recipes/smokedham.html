Smoked Ham

The quickest way to move past a lousy store bought ham is to make your own. The difference is just undeniable!

A homemade, whole smoked ham may seem like a daunting task, but the process couldn't be more simple. Follow this recipe to really make some memmories at you next holiday party.

Ingredients

  • 1x whole (apx. 8-10 lbs) Ham
  • 8-L Hot Water
  • 320g Kosher Salt
  • 360g White Sugar
  • 360g Brown Sugar
  • 8g Crushed Red Pepper Flakes
  • 5g Black Peppercorns
  • 16x Bay Leaves
  • 10g Fennel Seeds
  • 1x Medium Onion
  • 80g Garlic, Smashed
  • 64g Pink Curing Salt

Steps

  1. In a large container, combine the hot water with all of the ingredients
  2. Using a whisk, stir until all of the salt and suger has desolved
  3. Move the brine into the fridge and allow to completely cool
  4. Using an injector, inject the ham with some of the strained brine being sure it is evenly destributed
  5. Submerge the whole ham and refrigerate in the brine for a total of 10 days, flipping half way through
  6. After 10 days, remove the ham from the brine and tie it tightly
  7. Smoke the ham at 225F until the internal temperature reaches 155F (about 8 hours)
  8. Cool in the refrigerator until completely chilled
  9. Slice and enjoy!
